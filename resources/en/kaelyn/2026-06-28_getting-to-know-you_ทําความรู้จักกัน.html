Role-Play 1: The Sports Symposium

🤝
Okay, for our first scenario, let's imagine we're at an international sports coaching symposium.
☕
Context: We're at a coffee break during a major conference. Everyone is networking and discussing the latest trends in coaching and athletic performance.
👥
Roles:
Person A: An up-and-coming coach looking to make connections and learn from the best.
Person B: A veteran coach with experience across different disciplines and countries.
🎯
Success Goal: To build a professional rapport and exchange contact information for potential mentorship or collaboration.
🗣️
Useful Phrases:
Examples
- I was really struck by your point on...
- What's your take on the recent shift towards [e.g., data-driven coaching]?
- I'm looking to transition into...
- It's a field that's constantly evolving, isn't it?
- How do you approach the mental aspect of training with your athletes?

Role-Play 2: The Expat Coffee Chat

🇹🇭
Okay, next scenario. This one is about your interest in moving to Thailand.
☕
Context: You've connected online with an expat who has lived in Bangkok for years. You're meeting for coffee to pick their brain about the realities of living here.
👥
Roles:
Person A: A prospective expat, curious about the real pros and cons.
Person B: A seasoned expat, ready to offer candid advice.
🎯
Success Goal: To gain a realistic, nuanced understanding of the pros and cons of moving to Thailand to help make an informed decision.
🗣️
Useful Phrases:
Examples
- What's the reality of... versus the perception?
- If you were in my shoes, what's one thing you'd do differently from the start?
- What are some of the unwritten rules of navigating daily life here?
- Beyond the tourist spots, what's the local lifestyle really like?
- How does one go about finding a community?

Checking & Discussion Questions

🤔
What would be your biggest apprehension about moving to a new country?
💡
What do you think is the most common misconception people have about Thailand?
🌐
What's the difference for you between 'cultural integration' and 'maintaining your own identity' when living abroad?

Role-Play 3: The Human Library

📖
Our final scenario is a bit different. Have you ever heard of a 'Human Library,' where real people are the 'books' you can 'check out' to hear their stories?
🌍
Context: We're at a Human Library event, and you've chosen to check out a book titled 'The Global Nomad: Tales from Five Countries.' I'll be the book.
👥
Roles:
Person A (Cake): The 'Human Book' ('The Global Nomad')
Person B (You): The inquisitive 'Reader'
🎯
Success Goal: For the 'Reader' to gain a deep insight into the 'Book's' journey, motivations, and lessons learned by asking thoughtful questions.
🗣️
Useful Phrases (for the Reader):
Examples
- What was the catalyst for that chapter of your life?
- How did that experience reshape your worldview?
- Looking back, what's the common thread that ties all these experiences together?
- Tell me about a time you felt completely out of your element.
- Which place challenged you the most, and what did it teach you?

Speaking Tasks

🔄
Task 1: Role Reversal. Pick one of the first two scenarios we did. Let's switch roles and do it again. Notice how the conversation shifts from the other perspective.
📖
Task 2: Your 'Human Book' Title. If you were a 'Human Book', what would your title be? Come up with a title that reflects your story. Then, for 5 minutes, I'll be the 'Reader' and check you out.
🤸‍♀️
Task 3: Scenario Fusion. Imagine the veteran coach from Scenario 1 is ALSO the seasoned expat from Scenario 2. You bump into them at a gym in Bangkok. How would that conversation start and unfold?
